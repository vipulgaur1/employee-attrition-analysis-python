## 📌 Problem Statement

Employee attrition leads to increased hiring costs, productivity loss, and business instability.
This project analyzes employee data to identify key factors driving attrition and provide actionable insights to improve retention strategies.

---

## 📊 Dataset

IBM HR Analytics Employee Attrition dataset (~1,400 employees)

---

## 🛠 Tools & Technologies

* Python (Pandas, NumPy)
* Data Visualization: Matplotlib, Seaborn
* Jupyter Notebook

---

## 🔍 Project Overview

This project performs end-to-end exploratory data analysis (EDA) to understand patterns in employee attrition.
The analysis focuses on identifying high-risk employee segments and uncovering relationships between job roles, compensation, workload, and satisfaction levels.

---

## 📊 Key Analysis Performed

* Data cleaning and preprocessing (handling missing values, removing irrelevant columns)
* Univariate and bivariate analysis
* Attrition segmentation by department, income, overtime, and work-life balance
* Correlation analysis of numerical features
* Visualization of patterns using multiple plots

---

## 🎯 Top Drivers of Attrition

1. Overtime workload
2. Poor work-life balance
3. Low compensation roles

---

## 🚨 Key Insights

* Overtime employees show ~3x higher attrition compared to non-overtime employees
* Poor work-life balance leads to ~30% attrition, the highest among all groups
* Sales and Human Resources departments have the highest attrition rates
* Low-income roles such as Sales Representative and Laboratory Technician show significantly higher churn
* Younger employees tend to leave more frequently compared to experienced employees

---

## 💡 Business Recommendations

* Reduce overtime dependency through better workforce planning
* Improve work-life balance policies to reduce burnout
* Provide incentives and retention bonuses for high-risk roles
* Focus retention strategies on low-income and early-career employees
